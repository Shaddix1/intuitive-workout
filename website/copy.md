And for a while, it worked. Until it didn't.

Maybe you burned out. Maybe life got in the way. Maybe you just stopped caring — and then felt guilty about that.

## PHILOSOPHY SECTION (Solution)

**Section heading:**
What if your body already knows what it needs?

**Body:**
I spent years studying sports science in Vienna, then coaching real people through real life. Then I traveled — South America, Asia — and watched how people in very different cultures relate to their bodies. I came home with a different question.

What if the most effective workout isn't the most optimized one?
What if it's the one you actually *want* to do?

Intuitive Workout is built on one observation: the motivation that comes from inside — caring about the movement itself, not the deadline — outlasts every program I've seen.

It doesn't mean going easy. It doesn't mean skipping the barbell. I still love the classical Arnold pump. I still lift iron the old-school way.

It means learning to tell the difference between what your body needs and what a spreadsheet says it should need. Then building a practice around that.

**Three pillars (feature blocks):**

**Sustainability over optimization**
The fastest path to results you'll quit is one that ignores how you actually feel. The sustainable path is slower, less dramatic, and far more likely to last a lifetime.

**Listening over following**
Your body sends signals constantly — energy, desire, reluctance, fatigue. Learning to read those signals is a skill. This book teaches it.

**Your practice, not mine**
I won't give you a program. I'll give you the tools to build one. Or not — and still move well.

## FAQ

**Q: Is this an anti-fitness book?**
A: No. I love training. I still lift weights, run, and push myself hard when the moment calls for it. This book is pro-movement — it's anti-dogma. There's a difference.

**Q: Do I need any fitness experience to read this?**
A: None. Whether you've never been to a gym or you've been training for twenty years, the philosophy is relevant. The questions it asks don't require a fitness background to answer.

**Q: Is there a workout plan included?**
A: Deliberately, no. That would defeat the point. There are examples of how I approach my own training — not as blueprints, but as illustrations. The goal is to help you find your own approach.

**Q: Is this science-based or is it just vibes?**
A: Both, honestly — and I think that's the right answer. The motivation science is real. The adherence data is real. The body awareness frameworks come from research. But the lived experience matters too, and I don't pretend otherwise.

**Q: Is this a philosophy book or a practical guide?**
A: It leans toward philosophy, but with practical ground under it. Every idea connects to something you can actually do — or stop doing.
